About my cosplay and behind the scenes...


If you'd like to know more about my cosplay, you are in luck! I documented all parts of it with detail on how I did each step!


Before I begin with my ramble I'd like to give a huge shoutout to my mom and dad! This project wouldn't have been possible without them. My dad is the mastermind behind the wiring of the motion tracker, and my mom helped me a ton with the sewing of the flightsuit!

Flightsuit

I think the flightsuit was one of the hardest part of my cosplay. I ordered a flightsuit, hoping it would come in my size ; and after a longer delay it arrived, in a size I couldn't wear. I was quite dissapointed, and with the deadline growing closer, I knew there was no time to order a new one. So, we decided to do some....tweaks.


We realized that our best bet would be seperating the upper and lower area, and sew the top of the flightsuit onto the jenim that's below the flightsuit. I also made an "alien-claw-like" pattern on the back, so there would be more space to stretch the material. This worked out fine.

Next, I sewed the pins onto the flightsuit. The wings I got from ebay, the name tag I got customized, and the special globe patterned pin was commissioned from a friend that's learning embroidery (shoutout to Jusauria).


After this, I bought a second material that would fill the hole between the two seperated flightsuit parts. I'm actually very proud of this find, and to make it fit, I even sewed a zipper under it to fit the upper part.


The last part was sewing on the wool on the sides of the arms, which my mom managed to do wonderfully. Overall I'm proud of what we created compared to what we had to work with! Of course jumping into this project, I knew it was gonna be harder for me to manage due to my body shape, but I feel like it turned out fine in the end.

Motion tracker

If you are visiting this section of my website, I'm assuming this is the part you're most interested in. To be honest, it's also the project I'm proudest of. It turned out just as I wanted it to, if not even better, and I learnt a lot of new stuff during the creation of it.


A while ago I saw this amazing DIY project of a person creating their own alien based motion tracker with an Arduino Mega, some PIR sensors, an ultrasonic sensor, a buzzer and a display. Although I didn't have any use of it back then, the time for me to try my own hand at this project finally arrived.


Pretty much all the starter hardware instructions along with the shopping list come from this post on Instructables. I'd like to give a shoutout to Dylan91 because without him, we wouldn't have known where to start with this. My dad helped me setup the basics, and although I didn't understand everything he tried to explain, I did learn some stuff about electronics that I hadn't known before. I have always been a software person, and him as a hardware person we made the perfect team to complete this project.


Once he helped me set everything up, I downloaded the code and libraries from Dylan's post. Foolish me thinking code from 10 years ago would run on my freshly made build. Although not sure of the cause, the library 'SWTFT' simply didn't work with my cosplay and as I spent hours trying to find a solution, I came to the realization that this was a lost cause...but I couldn't just give up, could I?


Despite no previous experience in programming for Arduino devices, or programming anything with sensors in general, I decided to take on a challenge. If I was gonna go down, I wouldn't go down without putting up a fight. I decided to take a peak at Dylan's code to understand the basics of the functions he used, and I got to working....


Although I had a lot of doubt, I picked up the logic behind programming for the Arduino pretty quickly. I even decided to go all out and recreated the exact layout of the motion tracker from Alien Isolation! I am quite proud of how it turned out, and with the only issue being some eaten lines (I couldn't find a solution, besides just putting a vector as the background for flickering when redrawing elements when the dots draw over the lines, so I decided to go with a method that eats part of the lines but comes with no flickering), the layout was pretty for the sensors to be implemented.


Here is an explanation of how the sensors work ; there is a PIR sensor in the front, left and right side of the motion tracker. They each take up 1/3 of the field on the screen, respectfully to their sides. Once a signal is detected, a dot is drawn on a point of the side of the signal. After this the ultrasonic sensor is used to calculate a rough distance of the motion, and this is how the height position of the dot is determined. Sadly this position is often the same, so there is an addition of a low random number to spice things up a bit. Unfortunately I can't track where exactly motion is on a side, so the width position of the dot is determined by a random number (all carefully limited ; there are 3 sections in each line, I made sure a dot can't go too out of the border no matter where it is, but use it's sections full potential). With this, the motion tracker is functional and you get a rough estimately accurate position on the field at all times.


Lastly my dad did all the wiring and putting together of the junction boxes, and personally? I think this project turned out AMAZING. I couldn't have asked for a better prop! Shoutout to my dad for all his help on this one.
